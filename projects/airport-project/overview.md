# Airport Project — Overview

## Problem Context

Airport expansion projects involve dozens of electronic subsystems from multiple vendors, installed by multiple contractors. Without rigorous quality assurance oversight, systems may be installed incorrectly, without proper testing, or without meeting aviation regulatory standards — creating safety risks for passengers, aircraft, and airport operations.

## Fariz's Mission

To ensure all electronic systems in the Sultan Hasanuddin International Airport expansion meet international aviation standards, quality requirements, and contractual specifications through systematic supervision, testing, and commissioning oversight on behalf of PT Angkasa Pura I (airport owner).

## Scope of Work

Fariz oversees quality assurance for the following electronic system categories in the expansion project:

1. **Data Network Infrastructure** — Cisco-based LAN/WAN network systems
2. **PLC & SCADA — Baggage Handling System (BHS)** — Allen-Bradley PLCs for automated baggage conveying and sorting
3. **Fire Alarm System (FAS)** — Notifier brand fire detection and alarm coverage
4. **Public Address System (PAS)** — TOA and Bosch PA systems
5. **X-Ray Hold Baggage Screening (HBS)** — Leidos HBS X-ray screening system

## Target Beneficiaries

- **PT Angkasa Pura I** — airport owner; receives properly commissioned, standards-compliant systems
- **Future airport passengers** — benefit from safe, reliable baggage handling and emergency systems
- **Airport operations team** — receives systems with complete documentation for future maintenance

## Key Activities

- Witnessing Factory Acceptance Tests (FAT) and Site Acceptance Tests (SAT) for all electronic subsystems
- Reviewing contractor technical submittals and commissioning plans
- Cisco network configuration and troubleshooting support
- Documenting non-conformances and coordinating corrective actions

## Project Scale

Sultan Hasanuddin International Airport is the primary international gateway for Eastern Indonesia (Sulawesi, Maluku, Papua). The expansion project represents a major national infrastructure investment.
