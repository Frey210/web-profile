# Personal Branding

## Brand Persona

**"The Builder at the Edge"**

Fariz Achmad Faizal's professional persona is the **Grounded Innovator** — an engineer who operates at the intersection of physical systems (hardware, sensors, industrial machinery) and digital platforms (cloud APIs, mobile apps, monitoring dashboards). He is not a theorist; he is a builder who has shipped production systems, managed quality in a real airport, and gotten his hands dirty with both Cisco switches and custom PCB firmware.

The brand voice is: **authoritative but approachable**, **technically deep but practically focused**, **ambitious but grounded in real execution**. He earns credibility through demonstrated output — repositories, deployed products, competition wins, and published research — not credentials alone.

Secondary persona traits:
- **Startup CTO mindset** — thinks in products, not features; systems, not scripts
- **Safety-first engineering** — shaped by aviation QA experience where shortcuts are unacceptable
- **Cross-domain thinker** — connects aquaculture, aviation, ergonomics, and civic tech through a unified lens of IoT + automation

## Visual Identity Notes

**Aesthetic Direction:** Dark, High-Tech, Precise

- **Color Palette:** Deep navy / midnight blue primary, with electric teal or cyan accents, against near-black backgrounds. Reflects the aerospace and IoT domain — technical, trustworthy, cutting-edge.
- **Typography:** Clean, modern sans-serif (e.g., Inter, Space Grotesk, or Sora). Headings feel precise and engineered; body text is highly readable.
- **Iconography:** Thin-line or outlined icons. Circuit board, signal wave, and node/network motifs used sparingly as accent elements.
- **Imagery:** Technical photography — circuit boards, server racks, airport infrastucture, sensor arrays. Code on dark terminal backgrounds. No generic stock photos.
- **Motion:** Subtle, purposeful animations — data flowing through nodes, code typing effects on headings. Nothing decorative for its own sake.
- **Mood Board Keywords:** Mission Control · Terminal Interface · Precision Engineering · Dark Matter · Signal Processing

## Core Messaging

Three things every visitor should take away:

1. **"He ships real systems."**
   Not prototypes that never leave a lab — production deployments in aquaculture monitoring, airport infrastructure, and startup platforms used by real clients.

2. **"He bridges a rare gap."**
   Most engineers are either hardware people OR software people. Fariz operates natively in both domains: PLC/SCADA in an airport by day, Node.js APIs and Docker deployments for his startup in the evening.

3. **"He builds with purpose."**
   Whether monitoring shrimp ponds for farmers, ensuring airport safety compliance, or winning national research grants for innovative ideas — the work always serves a real-world need.
