# Engineering Philosophy

## 1. Bridging the Atoms and Bits
Fariz believes that the most impactful engineering happens at the intersection of physical hardware and digital software. His approach is rooted in the belief that an engineer should be conversant in the "language of atoms" (sensors, circuits, industrial machinery) and the "language of bits" (APIs, cloud infrastructure, containerization). This duality allows for the creation of systems that are truly integrated and responsive to the physical environment.

## 2. Safety-First Innovation
Influenced by his experience in airport quality assurance (PT Angkasa Pura I) and air navigation (AirNav Indonesia), Fariz adheres to a "Safety-First" innovation model. Whether building a startup prototype or commissioning an airport baggage handling system, he prioritizes reliability, fault tolerance, and rigorous testing. Innovation should never come at the cost of system integrity, especially in critical infrastructure.

## 3. Pragmatic Problem Solving
Engineering is a tool for solving real-world human problems. Fariz focuses on identifying the core pain points—whether it's a shrimp farmer's lack of water visibility or an ATC officer's fatigue—and building the most pragmatic, effective solution. He avoids "technology for technology's sake," instead selecting tools (like LoRa for remote sensing or Docker for easier deployment) based on their ability to deliver value and maintainability.

## 4. Scalable Foundations
Even for small-scale prototypes, Fariz designs with scalability in mind. This means utilizing containerization, building well-documented REST APIs, and following clean code principles. A system built on a scalable foundation can grow from a university project into a commercial startup product with minimal structural friction.

## 5. Continuous Observability
"You cannot manage what you do not measure." Fariz integrates monitoring and observability into everything he builds. From heartbeat signals in IoT firmware to Grafana dashboards for server infrastructure, he ensures that systems are transparent and self-reporting. This proactive stance reduces downtime and informs future technical decisions.

## 6. Commitment to Documentation
Fariz views documentation as an essential part of the engineering product, not an afterthought. High-quality documentation (system specifications, PRDs, architecture diagrams) ensures that knowledge is preserved, teams are aligned, and AI-ready knowledge bases can be built to accelerate future development.
