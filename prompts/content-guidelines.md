# Content Guidelines

## Principles
1. **Clarity over Cleverness**: Use direct, technically accurate language.
2. **Show, Don't Just Tell**: Use metrics (GPA 3.79, 10+ certifications) and specific results (Production-deployed, peer-reviewed) to prove expertise.
3. **Professional yet Approachable**: Maintain a senior engineer's authority but stay relatable as a "builder" who loves to tinker.

## Formatting Standards
- Use bullet points for readability.
- Use **bold text** for key technologies and tangible results.
- Keep paragraphs short (2-4 sentences).
- Use H2/H3 hierarchy consistently.

## Tone Check
- **Good:** "Built a production-grade IoT platform using ESP32 and MQTT to monitor 6+ water quality parameters in real-time." (Specific, result-oriented, technical).
- **Bad:** "I worked on an IoT project that was very successful and used cool technology to help farmers." (Vague, lacks technical depth, unprofessional).
- **Good:** "Operating at the intersection of atoms and bits, bridging industrial infrastructure with cloud-native startup solutions." (Branded, professional, unique).
- **Bad:** "I am an electrical engineer who also does some website stuff and robotics." (Undervalues the dual-domain expertise).
